Longboarding


Longboarding is a form of skateboarding that's done on a board longer than a traditional skateboard and bigger, softer wheels.

Compared to its parent, longboarding is more centered around smooth transportation and flowing movements.

I've been longboarding since I was in highschool, typically just for transporting but just doing that interested me. Let me give you a bit of info about longboarding!


Basic Longboarding Techniques

Pushing

  • Main way to gain speed on a longboard
  • Take back foot off of the board and propel yourself forward
  • More of a weight-tranfer move than you would think.
  • Tip: swing your arms for easier balance

Carving

  • The move longboarding is typically known for, it's textbook, surf-like movement
  • Turning the board by shifting your weight from one side to the other in smooth, s-shaped turns
  • Shift your weight to the front foot to turn left, and shift to the back foot to turn right

Foot Braking

  • Easiest way to slow down and stop on longboards
  • Take back foot of the board and drag it on the ground
  • keep most of your weight on your front foot, still on the board

Skateboarding vs Longboarding

Skateboarding

  • Smaller board with stiffer, smaller wheels
  • Stiffer trucks for more stabilities during tricks
  • rougher ride due to stiff parts, more for tricks and smooth pavement (skateparks)

Longboarding

  • Longer board with soft, wide wheels
  • Looser trucks for more carving and turning
  • Smoother ride with looser and softer parts, made for cruising and commuting. Soft wheels can take most impact compared to normal skateboard.

Downhill Skating

Downhill longboarding is a high-speed style of skating that is the complete opposite of longboarding's typical cruising nature.

Using perfect technique, skaters go down steep slopes at upwards of 60 mph.

This style of longboarding takes months of practice and years of experience to be safe. Due to it's high speeds, there's a very high chance of injury.
