Ice Skating


Ice skating is a recreational sport/activity where a person glides across a surface of ice on specially designed shoes called ice skates.

Ice Skates are, at its simplest, boots with metal blades fitted at the bottom.

They're usually made stiff to mimic the feel of the blade being an extension of the skater's foot.


Beginner Skating Techniques

Starting Stance

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees bent
  • Lean your upper body a bit forward. Not too far, but standing straight increases the chance of falling backwards

Heres a few beginner techniques

  • Marching
    • In a starting stance, walk on the ice by lifting your foot and placing it forward on the ice. Just like a marching soldier.
  • T-Push
    • Put your feet in "V" shape and transfer your weight on to the ball of one foot.
    • Then simultaneously, put your weight onto the foot facing forward and push off in a perpendicular direction with the other skate! That's a simple glide!

Ice and Roller Skating Differences

Ice Skating

  • Skaters skate on a restricted ice surface
  • Ice skates utilize one blade with 2 edges
  • Ice skates can make sharper turns due to blade edges
  • Low friction surface makes for finer techniques

Roller Skating

  • Skaters can skate on almost any solid surface
  • Recreational roller skating typically use quad skates; 4 wheels in 2x2 formation
  • Due to lack of edges and more friction, roller skating requires wider turns, and less refined techniques.
  • Skating on surfaces like pavement and concrete can easially lead to scrapes and injuries

Buying Skates

Fitting Tips

  • Skates are typically 1 - 2 sizes below your actual shoe size
  • You want your toes to be close to your the tip of your skates, but not curling
  • I recommend using hockey skates for your first time, the toe picks on figure skates tend to trip newcomers more than help
  • Stiffer ankle support is better for beginners, as you develop ankle strength

Recommended Beginner Skates
