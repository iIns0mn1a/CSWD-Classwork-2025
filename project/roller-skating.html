Roller Skating


Roller skating is a sport activity where a person moves across (mainly) flat surfaces using boots with wheels attacked to the bottom called roller skates.

I've been skating for a few months now, I've learned a majority of the basics and am ready to give a bit of info based on my experience.


Inline vs Quad Skates

Quad Skates vs Inline Skates

Inline skates and quad skates are two different types of skates and they both have their advantages and disadvantages.

Feature Quad Skates Inline Skates
Wheels 4 wheels in a 2x2 layout 3-5 wheels in a straight line
Boot Quality Typically soft boots, more stylish More rugged, hard for rough terrain
Terrain More for indoor rinks, but can be used outside Better for outdoor skating and rough surfaces
Typical Use More for artistic skating and beginners, more balance for smoother skating Emphasizes speed, agility, and fitness. More used in roller hockey and urban skating

Basic Techniques

Roller Skating and ice skating actually share similarities when talking about technique.

Basic Strides

  • Basic v-start stride
  • Push off of one foot, glide with the other.
  • Bend knees for longer strides

T-stop

  • Very similar to ice skating's drag t-stop
  • Useful for slowing down for a plow stop
  • With 90% of your weight on your front foot, drag your back foot to slow.

Crossovers

  • Fundamentally just like ice-skating's crossovers
  • easier to balance with quad skates
  • Cross one foot over the other, then uncross legs.
  • lean into turn

Plow Stop

  • Fundamental stopping method
  • Mainly used to come to a complete stop
  • Spread legs, then angle them inwards like a snowplow
  • bring feet together to come to a complete stop.

Urban Skating

Urban skating is a form of roller skating done on city streets, sidewalks, and other public spaces.

Skating can range from simple cruising around cityscapes, to doing jumps and tricks off city architecture.


Urban skating takes alot of concentration due to the rapidly changing traffic and environment (especially when skating around people). While its been declinling in popularity, it still has a community that shows itself during group rides; events where dozens of skaters skate through streets escorted by police.
